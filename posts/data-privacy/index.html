Give Back My Data

May 11, 2022 4-minute read
Development • Infrastructure • Privacy
infrastructure • development • self-hosting • privacy

First, go watch John Oliver on data brokers. That segment opened my eyes to a problem I was aware of but had no idea of the scope. I immediately started researching how I could take better control of my data. It isn’t easy, and in some cases is impossible or will result in a major decrease in digital quality-of-life. So I’ve set out on this journey to see if cutting ties with organizations that sell my data is even possible, and I’m documenting it so you can try for yourself. I realize this blog will probably preclude me from working at most of the FAANG companies, but is that really such a bad thing?

This page is and will continue to be in progress.

Goals

  1. Maximize security and privacy
  2. Control/self-host my own data
  3. Fully open source or privacy-focused app replacements
  4. Devops treatment - backups, scripted provisioning, auth/ddos protection
  5. Fully documented, for free.
  6. No new purchases from any major corporation.

Companies to cut

We’ll start with major service providers. These will likely be the most painful as their services are low level and fundamental to other systems. For example, where do I host this blog? Where do I keep notes?

Amazon

Evil corp orange. They treat their warehouse workers like slaves and some have literally died. Everyone I’ve known that worked at AWS said it was the worst job of their career. AWS dominates the cloud market, and is that really a good thing?

  • shopping: this sucks, but buy/trade/barter things locally. RIP radio shack.
  • AWS: Digital Ocean, Wasabi
  • Audible: libby
  • Kindle: supernote, Remarkable, etc.
Apple

Evil corp white. Siri is definitely selling my conversations to data brokers, and Apple hasn’t had the best record for worker’s rights either.

  • phones: old pixel, graphene, lineage, purism::librem?
  • OS: pureOS, ubuntu/mint/pop?
  • siri: jasperpi? etc? nextcloud?
  • macbooks: frame.work, recycle old macbooks, flash os?
Facebook

Evil incarnate. Zuckerberg took Ready Player One as an instruction manual, and is generally not a very good guy. I only keep it around so my parent’s friends don’t think I’m dead. The best I can do right now is delete the apps from my phone. They have been known to listen to your conversations and track your app usage even on supposedly secured/sandboxed operating systems.

Google

Probably need a whole blog on why they suck. This will be the hardest by far in terms of quality of life.

  • Gmail: need something that can sort/filter/categorize??
  • Drive: Nextcloud + Wasabi
  • Calendar: ?
  • Google Voice: VoIP, numbers
  • apps: chrome, maps, google search, google voice, etc?
Intuit (Turbotax, Mint)

Intuit lobbies the government to keep the tax code complex and prevent easy internet filing so they can charge us more money. They also sell our financial data. Fuck them.

  • Turbotax: similar cost as a local CPA, so I just switched, and it was easier than ever.
  • Mint: budgeting
    • Mint isn’t even that useful for budgeting due to the clunky UX.
    • also a security issue because it has all my accounts logged in
    • self hosted alternative: firefly 3, nextcloud cospend
    • nothing available to automatically connect to my banks (firefly uses two api companies that don’t operate in the US)
Microsoft

Evil corp light blue. Pretty unfriendly mega-corp actions spread out over a very long time.

  • Github - not sure if I really need to get rid of this but not really happy about MSFT - gitlab? bitbucket? self hosted?
  • xbox game pass - cancelled, I already use steam, and don’t play enough games to justify the game pass.
Twitter

A generally toxic place that I can’t bring myself to quit. It acts as my major source of news about current events and topics relevant to my career/interests. Also, alternatives suffer for the same reasons nobody was able to unseat WoW for 10+ years. Automate, aggregate, reduce. Make the algorithm work for me instead of stealing my attention.
